AI-Driven Chip Substrate Shortage Prompts Samsung Electro-Mechanics Price Hike

FC-BGA Demand Officially Exceeds Supply by 50% Price Hike Negotiations Begin with Global Big Tech Seen as Securing "Super Supplier" Status Focus on Developing Ultra-High Voltage MLCCs Humanoid Camera Supply to Begin in Second Half

Finance|
Published 2026.04.30. 15:55:59
|
By Lee Seok-jin
||

The semiconductor substrate market has entered an unprecedented shortage phase amid the artificial intelligence (AI) data center boom. As demand for Samsung Electro-Mechanics' (009150.KS) flagship product, flip-chip ball grid array (FC-BGA), continues to outstrip production capacity by more than 50% with sell-out runs, the company has begun full-scale price hike negotiations with its global Big Tech customers. Moving beyond full capacity utilization to secure pricing power, substrate makers are now being described as "super suppliers."

Samsung Electro-Mechanics said during its first-quarter earnings conference call Wednesday that demand for FC-BGA used in AI servers and data centers currently exceeds its production capacity by more than 50%. This formalizes the supply-demand imbalance mentioned by CEO Chang Duck-hyun at the shareholders' meeting in March. One cause of the shortage is that as AI chips grow larger in area, the number of FC-BGA units produced from a single wafer sharply declines.

"Requests from existing customers to expand supply are pouring in, and considering new Big Tech volumes starting in the second quarter, it is impossible to meet demand with our current production capacity," Samsung Electro-Mechanics management said. Accordingly, the company is conducting price renegotiations, including price hikes, with major customers to reflect rising raw material prices for copper and gold as well as the tight supply-demand situation.

With profitability secured through price hikes, the company is also pursuing capacity expansion. Samsung Electro-Mechanics said in the conference call that this year's investment will more than double from the previous year. Compared to its fourth-quarter statement that investment "will increase year-on-year," the plan has become more concrete. Investment resources will be concentrated on expanding capacity for high-capacity multilayer ceramic capacitors (MLCCs) for AI servers and substrates for AI accelerators.

Focus on Technology Leadership Amid Substrate Boom

Technological achievements disclosed for the first time in the first quarter are also notable. Samsung Electro-Mechanics launched a new 47uF (microfarad) product with double the capacity of existing 22uF products, aiming to take an early lead in the AI server platform market. In addition, as the 800V (volt) power systems previously used in electric vehicles are being adopted in AI servers, the company is pursuing long-term supply contracts for high-margin high-voltage MLCCs of 1kV (kilovolt) or higher to maximize profitability.

Samsung Electro-Mechanics is expanding its reach beyond AI servers to the new frontiers of "physical AI" and aerospace. It has made a full-scale entry into the MLCC market for low-earth orbit (LEO) satellites. In the aerospace market, where more than 100,000 MLCCs are mounted per satellite, Samsung Electro-Mechanics has already begun transactions with leading global customers. Given the higher component volume and extremely high reliability requirements compared to electric vehicles, this is expected to become a new pillar of revenue growth.

Tangible results have also emerged in the humanoid robot sector. The company has secured small and thin camera module technology capable of precise object gripping and will begin supplying a top-tier global humanoid customer in the second half. Samsung Electro-Mechanics' components will serve as the hands and eyes of robots. For the commercialization of glass substrates, called a game changer in next-generation packaging, the company will complete the establishment of a joint venture (JV) within this year. Sampling work with Big Tech customers is progressing faster than expected, brightening prospects for its 2027 commercialization target.

The industry assesses that Samsung Electro-Mechanics has laid the foundation for an "earnings quantum jump" by diversifying its business structure from mobile-centric to AI servers, automotive electronics and aerospace through technological innovation.

Meanwhile, Samsung Electro-Mechanics is expected to continue solid earnings growth in the second quarter, with shipments and average selling prices (ASP) rising in tandem following the first quarter. Amid the trends of agentic AI and the transition to high-voltage power grids, Samsung Electro-Mechanics' position in the substrate market is expected to further strengthen.
