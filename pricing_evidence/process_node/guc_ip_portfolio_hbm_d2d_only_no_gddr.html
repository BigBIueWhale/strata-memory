    What Are Cookies?

    Cookies are small text files stored on your computer or mobile device by a website you visit. These text files are sent back to the website each time your browser requests a page, allowing websites to function, be more efficient, or remember your preferences to personalize your experience. Each cookie is unique to your browser and may contain information such as a unique identifier, website domain name, and certain data.

    Cookies may include:

    • First-party cookies: First party cookies are set directly by GUC.
    • Session cookies: Session cookies last only as long as your web browser is open and are deleted once you close your browser. Session cookies enable website navigation and maintain login status.
    • Persistent cookies: Persistent cookies are not deleted when your browser is closed. They remain on your device until they expire or you manually remove them. Persistent cookies enable tracking unique visitors to our site, page views, and web statistics.

    Types of Cookies We Use

    As indicated in our cookie banner we only use Strictly Necessary Cookies (Essential) cookies on the GUC website These cookies are essential for the operation and security of the website. They support secure sessions and help prevent cross-site request forgery (CSRF). Such cookies cannot be disabled and you cannot opt out of these cookies through our cookie banner.

    Types of Data We Collect

    To ensure the secure and proper operation of our website, we collect only the following information through strictly necessary cookies:

    • Session Information: Using the session cookie to identify users and maintain secure browsing sessions.
    • Request Verification Information: Using the XSRF-TOKEN cookie to verify request origins and protect against cross-site request forgery (CSRF).

    We do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking cookies and do not collect personalized browsing records or cross-site tracking data through cookies.
