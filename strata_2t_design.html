committed engineering design · codename STRATA‑2T

A two‑tier‑memory AI accelerator

1.79 TB/s and 2 TiB on one module — a literal RTX 5090‑class fast tier fronting a coherent 2 TiB bulk tier, specified to the part number, the pin budget, the picosecond, and the nanosecond.

Compute die: custom TSMC N3E, GB202‑class. Fast tier: 512‑bit GDDR7 (RTX PRO 6000 subsystem). Bulk tier: 8‑channel DDR5 3DS RDIMM. Method: specify, don’t speculate; every wall closed with a shipping existence‑proof; open questions flagged, not buried. Compiled 2026‑07‑18.

The requirement is exact and it is not the impossible one. Not “2 TiB of GDDR7 at 115 TB/s” (that dies on shoreline physics). The ask is 1.79 TB/s of bandwidth — one 5090’s worth — on a hot working set, with 2 TiB physically resident so a trillion‑parameter MoE runs. That is a tiering problem, and tiering is a shipping, measured art (NVIDIA Grace Hopper). This document commits every choice.

Verdict up front

Buildable today, from catalog parts, on ~6,000 balls.

A custom N3E die carries a native 512‑bit GDDR7 interface (96 GiB clamshell, 1,792 GB/s — byte‑identical to the RTX PRO 6000’s subsystem) and eight native DDR5 channels driving 2 TiB of 3DS RDIMM (8 × 256 GiB). One coherent address space of ~2.09 TiB. At ≤96 GiB working set it is a 5090/PRO 6000; past that, cold weights stream from the bulk tier. The signal integrity is two already‑shipping SI regimes composed side by side; the total ball count (~6,000) is proven by AMD’s socketed MI300A (LGA‑6096). Nothing here needs a new transistor or a new standard.

The key architectural fact that shapes everything: 1.79 TB/s is a single 512‑bit GDDR7 interface, and that interface caps at 96 GiB (clamshell 3 GB devices — the RTX PRO 6000 ceiling). So 2 TiB cannot be one uniform GDDR7 pool. It doesn’t need to be: the spec asks for 5090 bandwidth on the hot set and 2 TiB of residence, which is precisely a two‑tier machine.

Two tiers, one coherent pool. The fast tier lives on the accelerator module (GDDR7 must be millimetres from the die); the bulk tier lives in DIMM slots on the baseboard (DDR5 tolerates the reach). This is the NVIDIA Grace Hopper division of labour — HBM for bandwidth, a large coherent DRAM tier for capacity — with the tiers re‑cast as GDDR7 + DDR5.

05Tier 2 — the 2 TiB DDR5 bulk tier

2 TiB lands cleanly as 8 channels × 256 GiB (8 × 256 GiB = 2 TiB exactly). The controllers are native on the die (not CXL) so the tier is DRAM‑class latency and coherent as a NUMA node — the Grace approach, but with DIMMs for capacity and service.

Your buffering instinct — realised, legallyThree conversations ago the idea was “silicon that presents itself as higher‑capacity DDR.” That is exactly what a 256 GiB 3DS RDIMM is: an RCD re‑registers the command/address bus and TSV‑stacked (3DS) dies pack 256 GiB behind one channel. The buffering that was fatal on the GDDR7 fast tier (frozen, latency‑starved) is native and standard on the DDR5 bulk tier (latency‑tolerant). The instinct was right; it just belongs on this tier.

06Tier 2 signal integrity — registered, so the reach is legal

The bulk tier is standard server DDR5 SI — the reason DIMM slots on a baseboard work at DDR5‑6400 is the register. The RCD captures the command/address bus and re‑drives it fly‑by to the ranks, cutting the controller’s electrical load so the longer, socketed, multi‑rank net closes. The DQ lanes stay point‑to‑point and un‑buffered on an RDIMM (that is the RDIMM‑vs‑LRDIMM latency win). Every DDR5 server on earth is the existence proof.

  • Data path: two independent 32‑bit sub‑channels/DIMM; server ECC = 40 bit each (32 DQ + 8 CB) → 80 bit; BL16 → 64 B/sub‑channel.
  • CA path: host 7‑bit DDR edge CA → RCD de‑muxes to the 14‑bit SDR DRAM‑side CA; fly‑by to all ranks.
  • Mechanical: 288‑pin module, 133.35 × 31.25 mm, 0.85 mm contact pitch (JEDEC MO‑329, socket SO‑023).
  • Slot pitch ~7.5–8 mm typical [not a JEDEC constant — board choice].

07Coherence & the tiering engine

The two tiers are one hardware‑coherent address space, surfaced as two NUMA nodes — the GH200 model. Hot data is kept in GDDR7 by a hardware access‑counter migrator; the fast path is never buffered, which is what preserves the “5090 at ≤96 GiB” guarantee.

  • Address translation: a single per‑process page table (ATS), so the GPU can touch bulk‑tier lines coherently with no page‑fault handler in the path — exactly how Grace Hopper reaches remote LPDDR5X.
  • Migration: access‑counter promotion of hot 2 MB pages GDDR7←→DDR5 (Grace uses a threshold of 256). KV cache and always‑on weights are pinned to GDDR7; cold expert weights are demand‑streamed from DDR5.
  • Isolation of the fast path: the DDR5 controllers are physically separate blocks; a ≤96 GiB resident workload issues zero DDR5 traffic, so its latency/bandwidth are bit‑identical to a stand‑alone GDDR7 GPU.

The decisive latency choice is native DDR5 controllers over CXL: the bulk tier stays DRAM‑class (~150–250 ns GPU‑visible) rather than paying CXL’s serial ~400–550 ns under load. The bulk tier is slower than GDDR7, but only by a fraction — and only cold‑expert misses ever see it.

09Physical realization & ball budget

The form factor is forced by one measurement: a DDR5 DIMM is 133.35 mm long — taller than a PCIe card’s 111.15 mm full height — and a card has no host memory controller anyway. So 2 TiB of DIMMs cannot ride a PCIe card. STRATA‑2T is therefore an OCP OAM module (102 × 165 mm) that carries the die + GDDR7 clamshell on‑module, mounted onto a server baseboard whose DIMM field holds the 8 DDR5 slots — the natural home the industry already builds.

10Why this closes when “2 TiB of GDDR7” didn’t

The earlier analysis proved a single die cannot host 2 TiB of GDDR7 at full per‑chip bandwidth: 1,024 point‑to‑point devices demanded ~130,000 balls, ~115 TB/s across one shoreline, ~10 kW, and PAM3 reach that can’t span the parts. STRATA‑2T evades every one of those walls because it stopped asking for 115 TB/s:

Wall (the impossible design)Impossible versionSTRATA‑2T
Ball count~130,000~6,000 (fast tier only 512‑bit; bulk tier pin‑efficient 3DS RDIMM)
Shoreline BW~115 TB/s~1.79 TB/s hot + ~0.4–0.8 TB/s bulk
Memory devices on the fast, short net1,024 GDDR732 GDDR7 (a 5090’s count)
Bulk capacity attachimpossible (GDDR7 caps at 96 GiB)3DS RDIMM: 256 GiB behind each of 8 channels

The bulk capacity moved off the hard, short, wide GDDR7 net and onto a latency‑tolerant, registered, TSV‑stacked DDR5 net — which is precisely the memory technology built to pack terabytes behind a handful of pins. That single relocation is the whole design.

The load‑bearing correctionKimi uses MLA, not MHA, so its KV cache caches a single joint latent — (kv_lora_rank + qk_rope_head_dim) = 576 elements per token per layer, no ×2 and no ×heads. The naive formula gives ~262 GB at 128K; MLA gives 4.29 GiB. Size the KV tier with the MLA formula or you over‑provision by ~28×.

13Open questions & unverified items

As a pre‑silicon decision record: the plan is internally consistent and every wall has a shipping existence‑proof, but these specific numbers are estimates or gated behind paywalled standards and must be pinned before tape‑out.

  • GDDR7 eye height (mV) / width (ps) and the hard max trace length (mm) / insertion‑loss (dB) budget live in gated JESD239; the ≤45 mm reach and ~53.6 ps UI are computed/estimated.
  • GDDR7 pJ/bit & per‑device watts (~4–5.5 pJ/bit, ~4–6 W) are estimates — only % efficiency claims are public.
  • DDR5 RCD/DB absolute‑ns latency: only “RCD ~1 nCK” is solid; the DB adder (~1.5 ns) and the GPU‑visible DDR5 figure (~150–250 ns) are estimates for this configuration.
  • Exact 256 GiB PN: Samsung M321RBJA0M22-CLP is verified for capacity/speed/rank (die derived); no clean Micron/SK Hynix 256 GiB PN was confirmable. 256 GiB MRDIMM is a tall/double‑height ~20 W module — check the 31.25 mm envelope.
  • Migration hit‑rate for fine‑grained MoE (384 experts, 8 active) can be as low as ~33–44% in adversarial cases; the design leans on pinning always‑on + KV and on CXL‑3.2‑style hot‑page monitoring / access‑counter migration.
  • N3E ~800 mm² monolithic: no shipping N3E die is that large; if logic+SRAM overflow the reticle, split to chiplets on CoWoS‑L (3.5× reticle shipping).

STRATA‑2T is one custom N3E die that is a 5090 when the work is small and a 2 TiB memory server when the work is Kimi‑sized — a 96 GiB GDDR7 fast tier at 1,792 GB/s soldered clamshell‑short beside the die, and 2 TiB of registered, TSV‑stacked DDR5 hanging off eight native channels on the baseboard, fused into one coherent address space. The fast tier’s SI is the shipping RTX PRO 6000; the bulk tier’s SI is every DDR5 server; the ~6,000‑ball package is AMD’s MI300A. It needs no new memory type, no new standard, and no new transistor — only the discipline to put each tier where its physics allows it to live.
