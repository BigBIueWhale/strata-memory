Is anyone building STRATA-V?

A far-and-wide hunt for a device-mode, commodity-DRAM-fronting, GPU-impersonating converter — and what the answer says about this design.

Compiled from a wide research pass (2026-07): vendor/investor disclosures, patents (USPTO/WO/CN), arXiv, and trade press, cross-checked across channels; ~154 source artifacts archived in pricing_evidence/prior_art/. Product names, dates, and figures are as disclosed by the parties and press; company performance numbers are claims unless marked. Confirmed-vs-reported ledger in §07.

STRATA-V’s distinguishing move is not “cheap DRAM for inference” — that is now a crowded, well-funded thesis. It is one specific act: a converter that impersonates a standard GDDR7 device to a GPU. This record asks whether anyone builds that, and reads the answer honestly — including the part that vindicates the design.

Verdict

Nobody builds the impersonation. The thesis around it is crowded. And it’s unclaimed not because it can’t be done — we proved it’s within spec — but because a shipped GPU’s controller is frozen.

The device-mode front — silicon that answers a GPU’s memory training and presents a JEDEC GDDR7 device interface to it — is claimed by no company, patent, or paper found anywhere. But that gap is not an electrical dead end: Part II of strata_v_design.html establishes the impersonation of the 512-DQ front is byte-identical and deterministic, within spec on latency and signal integrity. Give the RTX 5090 its due: its 512-lane interface is provably impersonable — only its shipped, frozen controller says no, and design-in removes that.

01The five axes — and the one that is the signature

Every candidate was scored on how much of the idea it actually implements:

AxisWhat it means
A1 convertera separate converter/ASIC between accelerator and memory
A2 commodity DRAMbacked by ordinary DDR5/LPDDR, not new media or proprietary stacks
A3 VRAM-class BW~1.79 TB/s (RTX-5090-class 512-bit GDDR7) via massive channel interleave
A4 multi-TiB depthcapacity from depth, not width
A5 impersonationthe signature: answer memory training and present a standard GDDR/HBM device interface to a GPU

A1–A4 are the economics and mechanism. A5 is the move — and the whole question is who, if anyone, does A5.

02The verdict, split cleanly

  • A5 — impersonating an unmodified GPU — is unclaimed. No company, lab, or patent (US, China, Israel, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, EU) builds a device-mode GDDR/HBM converter that lets an unmodified merchant GPU see commodity DRAM as its own VRAM at ~1.8 TB/s. The research also independently confirmed our own finding: no commercial device-mode GDDR/HBM PHY IP exists — only host-mode.
  • A1–A4 — commodity DRAM fanned out by custom silicon at HBM-class bandwidth for inference — is a crowded, well-funded field. At least six credible efforts, one already shipping. Every one owns or co-designs the host side (their own SerDes / die-to-die / optical port, or their own accelerator) instead of impersonating a JEDEC device to someone else’s GPU.

The pattern is uniform: the economics is validated by serious money, the bandwidth mechanism is real, and everyone routes around the GPU’s native memory interface rather than speaking it. A5 is the empty column.

The closest shipping analog — and why it’s not in this tableCerebras MemoryX is the one architecture at hyperscale built on the same A1/A2/A4 economics — commodity DRAM (now literally “commodity DDR5 in 1U servers”), capacity decoupled from compute, one weight copy shared across many engines. It sits outside this peer table because it isn’t a converter at all: it doesn’t do A3 (per-node egress is ~150 GB/s, not VRAM-class) and doesn’t do A5 — it deletes the random-access contract and streams weights over Ethernet, then abandons even that for on-wafer SRAM when latency matters. It’s the strongest proof the economics is real and that the impersonation niche is empty. Full record: cerebras_memoryx.html.

04The two directions of special interest

OpenAI — the opposite of the idea. Its custom inference chip (“Jalapeño,” with Broadcom, announced mid-2026) does not disclose its memory config, but OpenAI’s patents are HBM-maximalist — a bridge architecture for up to 20 real HBM stacks (WO 2026/075822) — and it has locked up enormous HBM/DRAM-wafer supply. It is buying the most HBM anyone ever has, not substituting commodity DRAM. [chip confirmed; memory config not disclosed; exclusive-HBM-supply reports are press-level]

China — builds HBM itself, doesn’t impersonate it. The response to export controls is domestic HBM: Huawei’s self-developed HiBL/HiZQ stacks (128 GB @ 1.6 TB/s shipping; 144 GB @ 4 TB/s coming) and CXMT’s domestic HBM ramp. Deep zh-CN sweeps (自研HBM / 堆叠DRAM / 存算一体) found no “DDR-fronted-as-HBM converter” program anywhere.

05The reconciliation — why this vindicates the design

The tempting reading of §01–04 is bleak: the one thing nobody does (A5, impersonate an unmodified GPU) is the very thing this project’s own rtx5090_strata_v_retrofit_verdict.html calls impossible on a stock card. That reading conflates two different walls, and prising them apart is the whole point.

The electrical wall does not exist — we proved itPart II of strata_v_design.html (§08–11) establishes from first principles that impersonating the 512-DQ GDDR7 front is within spec: the data path is byte-identical to a 5090 (same 512 pins, 28 Gb/s PAM3, 1.79 TB/s); capacity is extended on the slow CA bus (+6 bits, electrically free); and the fixed-beat read contract is honored deterministically by construction (you author the deadline — §10’s two paper checks). Electrically, in latency, and in signal integrity, the impersonation holds. That is a positive result: it proves a 5090-class 512-lane interface could host this. The GPU’s own memory interface — the thing every peer abandons — is demonstrably impersonable.

The wall that does exist is the frozen stock controller — a design-in problem, not a physics one. A shipped 5090 can’t be tricked because its controller is frozen at 32 GiB with no way to name more, its timing is training-frozen, and 28 Gb/s PAM3 can’t leave the board (the four walls of the retrofit verdict). None of those are failures of the impersonation; they are properties of a finished card. A 5090-class device whose controller is co-designed to issue the extended addresses and accept the relaxed-but-fixed timing dissolves every one of them.

Where STRATA-V actually sitsIts buildable, design-in form is a peer of Credo / Majestic / Celestial / Eliyan — same commodity-DRAM-for-inference economics. But it is the only design in the field that keeps the GPU’s native memory interface instead of routing around it, and the only one that has shown that interface can be impersonated within spec. The others own a host link precisely to avoid the impersonation; STRATA-V proves you don’t have to. What’s unclaimed (A5) is unclaimed because of frozen controllers, closed training firmware, warranty/qual, and a dense patent thicket — not because the electricals fail.
The freedom-to-operate caveat, kept honest“Unclaimed” is not “unpatented territory.” The impersonation lane is densely claimed — MetaRAM (now Google) “Simulating a memory circuit” (US 8,949,519), Eliyan’s conversion chiplet (US 12,058,874), and Netlist / AMD / Innosilicon interleaving-buffer families. The specific GDDR7-device-to-a-GPU move is unclaimed, but a freedom-to-operate pass is mandatory before anyone builds it.

06What is genuinely open

  1. The device-mode front to a co-designed GPU (A5). Silicon that impersonates a standard GDDR7 device — answers training, presents fixed-beat deterministic reads — with interleaved commodity DDR5/LPDDR behind it. Proven within spec here; built by no one.
  2. Commodity DRAM behind a custom HBM4 base die. The industry already lets customer logic move into the HBM base die — but always fronting real HBM DRAM. Putting commodity DRAM behind that sanctioned front is the nearest legitimized on-ramp to A5, and is unproposed publicly.
  3. The consumer / prosumer angle. Every peer targets data-center xPUs; nobody targets giving an RTX-class GPU a multi-TiB VRAM illusion.

07Honest flags

  • Confirmed vs reported. Confirmed by primary disclosure: the existence of OpenAI’s chip, the OpenAI 20-HBM-stack patent, Credo Weaver, Majestic’s LPDDR pooling, Qualcomm HBC’s existence, Eliyan’s patent, Huawei’s HBM roadmap. Report-level (press, unconfirmed): OpenAI’s exclusive-HBM-supply and memory config, most per-chip bandwidth figures (company claims), CXMT ramp timing. Details and links in pricing_evidence/prior_art/.
  • Why A5 may be structurally unclaimed (not merely overlooked): closed GPU training firmware, warranty/qualification, device-mode PAM3 signal-integrity difficulty, latency vs. soldered VRAM, and the fact that everyone capable (Broadcom, Marvell, Credo, memory vendors) has higher-margin adjacent businesses. Our own analysis addresses the first two by design-in and the third/fourth by the §08–11 proof — but the market reasons are real.
  • Stealth risk. Absence of public evidence cannot rule out stealth efforts; several parties’ interconnect details are opaque.

Bottom line. The idea’s economics (commodity DRAM for frontier inference) is now consensus and crowded; its bandwidth mechanism (custom fanout silicon) is shipping or funded; its signature move — device-mode impersonation of a GDDR7 device to a GPU — is claimed by no one. But that last gap is the vindication, not the flaw: this project already proved the 512-lane front can be impersonated within spec, electrically and in latency and signal integrity. The RTX 5090’s interface could host this; only its shipped, frozen controller says no — and that is a controller you design, not a law of physics. Every peer routes around the GPU’s memory interface; STRATA-V is the one design that speaks it.
