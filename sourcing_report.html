Can you actually buy STRATA?

What of the STRATA designs — a GPU with ~2 TiB of coherent memory at ~1.79 TB/s — is purchasable today, as a product, as parts, or as a path to build.

Findings from four parallel research agents (turnkey products · DDR5 parts · converter silicon · CXL/appliance/chips). Prices observed ~2026‑07‑13/18 at the reachable resellers; many sites (eBay, amd.com, Alibaba, Mouser, Crucial) were bot‑blocked this session, so figures are bracketed and flagged. Prices are volatile in the 2026 DRAM crunch. Not quotes.

The four hunts converge on one picture: nobody sells the exact STRATA point — one GPU module, ~2 TiB, ~1.79 TB/s, one coherent space. But the market brackets it tightly from every side, the design’s own commodity‑DRAM thesis is already buyable as parts, and the custom converter is prototypeable now for ~$12k.

Verdict

Buy the pieces today; the exact chip needs a GPU‑vendor co‑design.

  • Closest thing you can order at a listed price: the NVIDIA GB300 “AI Station” (Supermicro ARS‑511GD‑NB‑LCC) — $80,541 — which is STRATA‑2T’s fast‑tier + coherent‑bulk‑tier shape (252 GB HBM3e @ 7.1 TB/s + 496 GB LPDDR5X), at ~⅓ the capacity.
  • The design’s commodity thesis, buyable as parts: a 2‑socket Xeon 6980P + 24× MRDIMM‑8800 ≈ 2.3 TB coherent @ ~1.69 TB/s for ~$110k — the STRATA‑V bandwidth‑from‑many‑channels idea, real — but it is a CPU, not a GPU.
  • The converter is prototypeable now on an Altera Agilex 7 M‑series kit (~$11.5k) with hardened DDR5; the literal 512‑pin fixed‑beat front only exists in a ~$50M+ N3E ASIC that (per our retrofit verdict) needs a GPU‑vendor partner.
  • Cost reality check: the 2 TiB of DDR5 alone is ~$37k–115k in the crunch — 6–10× what the STRATA‑V draft estimated.

03The converter — a buildable staircase, one un‑buyable block

The custom 3‑nm converter is realizable from catalog parts except its device‑mode front PHY. Nobody sells a device‑mode GDDR/DDR front (the block that answers training and launches on a frozen beat) — every FPGA and PHY IP is host/controller side. That is exactly the retrofit‑verdict wall.

04CXL & appliances — the productized “huge coherent memory”, buyable now

  • Astera Labs Leo A1000‑1254AB — a PCIe x16 CXL add‑in card, 4× DDR5‑5600 RDIMM slots, up to 2 TB per controller, ~50 ns adder — listed as orderable. The closest real silicon to the STRATA “converter”.
  • Marvell Structera X 2504 — 4‑ch DDR5, 8 DIMMs, >6 TB, 200 GB/s, CXL 2.0 (hyperscaler sampling).
  • H3 Platform Falcon C5022 — CXL pooling appliance, up to 5.5 TB, 22× E3.S, 8 hosts, “Order Now”. Liqid SmartStack — 10–40 TB CXL pools (RFQ).
  • Memory modules: Samsung CMM‑D 256 GB (RFQ), Micron CZ120 128/256 GB (OEM‑certified; current orderability unclear [flag]), SK hynix CMM‑DDR5 96 GB.
  • System integrators (call today): Thinkmate (in‑stock QuickShip), Exxact, Penguin Solutions (owns SMART Modular — can quote a CXL box end‑to‑end), AMAX, Broadberry, ICC.
  • Grey‑market graphics chips (AliExpress, verified live): GDDR7 K4VCF325ZC-SC28 (24 Gb) ~$42–70; GDDR6 K4ZAF325BM ~$12–26. A 96 GB clamshell set ≈ $1.4–3.1k — but useless for capacity without the controller (per the retrofit verdict). Authenticity unverifiable pre‑delivery (the same sellers list obvious fakes).

06What couldn’t be verified (tool access, not existence)

Web search budget was exhausted, so agents fetched constructed URLs directly; several hosts blocked that. Treat the following as unconfirmed this session: all eBay listings (timeouts) → no used GH200 / Mac Studio prices; amd.com (MI355X specs came from a partner spec sheet, not AMD); Alibaba (captcha) → no broker MOQ quotes; Mouser/Crucial/CDW/Octopart (blocked) → some distributor prices; every “quote‑only” and “analyst estimate” price (GB200 NVL4, MI355X node, NVL72 ~$3–4M, GPTshop); the exact config behind Wiredzone’s $35,401 GH200 SKU; N3E mask‑set (~$15–25M) and full‑program NRE (~$50M+) are industry estimates; grey‑market chip/DIMM authenticity is unverifiable pre‑delivery. Prices move weekly in the crunch.

Bottom line. The exact STRATA point — one GPU module, ~2 TiB, ~1.79 TB/s, one flat coherent space — is a genuine gap in the 2026 market: you can buy the architecture at less capacity (GB300 Station, $80k), or the memory numbers without a GPU (2S Xeon + MRDIMM, ~$110k), or exceed everything at rack scale ($3M+ NVL72). The converter that would close the gap is prototypeable now (~$12k FPGA) but its device‑mode front PHY is the one block no one sells — buildable only as a ~$50M+ N3E ASIC with a GPU‑vendor co‑design partner, exactly as the retrofit verdict concluded. And in this DRAM crunch, the memory alone costs 6–10× the draft estimate.
