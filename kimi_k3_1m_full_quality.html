02The precision determination — per generation, from evidence

“Full quality” = the model’s own native trained precision. Running above it is a phantom (more VRAM, zero quality); below it is over-quantizing. The Kimi line’s trajectory, each quoted from its downloaded card/config:

GenerationNative precisionEvidence
DeepSeek-V3 (lineage parent)block-FP8 e4m3“we only provide FP8 weights”
K2-Instruct (2025-07)block-FP8 e4m3“checkpoints are stored in the block-fp8 format” (958.5 GiB measured)

K2.5 / 2.6 / 2.7 (2026)INT4-QAT (same scheme) + BF16 vision“same native int4 quantization method as K2-Thinking” (K2.7: 554.3 GiB)

MXFP4 = OCP microscaling FP4: 4-bit E2M1 elements + one E8M0 scale per 32-block = ~4.25 bits/param effective. It is K3’s full quality — QAT’d, served, and benchmarked there ($3/$15-per-MTok economics only close at 4-bit). FP8 (~3.0 TiB) or FP16 (~5.9 TiB) would be decompression phantoms.

04The config & correctness

45 × Crucial CP48G56C46U5 (48 GiB DDR5-5600 UDIMM, official Micron brand, JEDEC-native CL46) = 2,160 GiB. Fits the entire 1.51–1.77 TiB range with ≥19% headroom (45×32 = 1,440 GiB fails even the low case). Bandwidth 45 × 44.8 GB/s = 2,016 GB/s raw → the front’s 1.79 TB/s at 88.9% duty.

Why 48 GiB — its 1.79 TB/s break-even is the matched one (and why not 64 GiB)

Bandwidth forces 45 channels (45 × 44.8 = 2,016 GB/s raw → 1.79 TB/s net); each channel takes one UDIMM, so capacity is simply 45 × density and the ~10-chip converter is identical at every rung. Each density has a break-even capacity — where its 45 sticks fill all 45 channels and the build first reaches 1.79 TB/s (the Capacity column below). Every rung is the same CL46 JEDEC-native part; only the stick — and thus the break-even — changes:

We chose 48 GiB because its break-even — 2.11 TiB (45 × 48) — is the smallest one that still clears K3’s ~1.56 TiB. The 64 GiB part (CP64G56C46U5) was the alternative and is identical where it counts — same CL46, same DDR5-5600, same bandwidth-per-channel — but its break-even is 2.81 TiB (45 × 64): ~0.7 TiB more capacity and cost for zero gain in bandwidth or latency. So 48-vs-64 is purely a break-even-capacity choice, not a performance one. And note what a break-even is: below it a given stick cannot reach 1.79 TB/s (drop a stick, drop a channel), so for the 48 GiB part full bandwidth and 2.11 TiB are welded to one point. To break even lower, step down the density dial to a smaller stick (24 GiB → 1.05 TiB), never a smaller converter (see strata_v_design.html §04).

06Honest flags

  • Pre-weights: K3 open weights land 2026-07-27; the MXFP4/BF16 byte-split, exact layer/KV geometry, and MTP presence are from the blog + lineage inference — firm within the 1.51–1.77 TiB range, exact at release.
  • MTP flagged: no K2-line model shipped an MTP module; the +17.8 GiB assumes K3 does. If not, subtract it.
  • Price: Crucial CP48G56C46U5 MSRP is a Wayback snapshot and the page flags EOL (Micron exited consumer Crucial DRAM) — a last-published paper floor; live/IL prices run higher. US retail was bot-walled.
  • Scope: DRAM only. The converter silicon (custom N3E ASIC ~$50M NRE, or FPGA prototype) is separate.

Bottom line. Kimi K3 is a real, announced 2.8 T, MXFP4-native, 1M-context, multimodal model. At its true full quality (native MXFP4 + un-quantized FP16 KV + MTP + vision) it needs ~1.56 TiB → 45 × 48 GiB DDR5-5600 UDIMM @ 1.79 TB/s ≈ ₪61,500 landed.
